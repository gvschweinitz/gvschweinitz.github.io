Biography

Welcome to my website! I am Assistant Professor for Economics, especially Quantitative Macroeconomics, at Leipzig University. At the same time, I am the head of the research group “Volatility, Growth and Financial Crises” at the Halle Institute for Economic Research, Member of the Leibniz Association. In my research, I use state-of-the-art econometric models that offer different solutions for the identification problem posed by the research question at hand. In my ongoing work, I focus on Bayesian inference in structural VAR models and the usage of large datasets to construct external instruments for macroeconomic shocks.

Interests

  • Economic crises
  • Business cycle dynamics
  • Early-warning models
  • Structural identification in empirical macroeconomics
